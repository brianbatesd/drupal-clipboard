​What is zoning?

Zoning defines an area's density, permitted uses and applicable standards for development per the City of Vancouver's Land Use and Development Code.

How can I find out what zoning designation a property is?

Zoning may be found by visiting Clark County Property Information Center.

What uses are allowed in a zoning designation?​

Each zoning designation has types of uses that are permitted outright, allowed as a limited use, conditional use or is prohibited.

Explore the allowed uses based on zoning designation:

  • Low Density Residential Districts (R-2, R-4, R-6, R-9) at VMC 20.410.030
  • High Density Residential Districts (R-18, R-22, R-30, R-35) at VMC 20.420.030
  • Commercial and Mixed Use Districts (CN, CC, CG, CX, WX, CPX. MX) at VMC 20.430.30
  • Industrial Districts (OCI, IL, IH, ECX) at VMC 20.440.030

Where can I find planning and building applications?

Land use applications can be found on the Land Use and Planning Applications page.

Residential building permit applications can be found on the Residential Building Permit Applications page.

Commercial building permit applications can be found on the Commercial Building Permit Applications page.

Where are my property lines?

Clark County Property Information Center may have a plat map to assist you in finding your property lines and/or dimensions. Ultimately it is up to the property owner to verify property line locations. In order to determine the precise location of your property lines, the services of a licensed professional land surveyor may be required.

What are the setbacks and building height for a specific zoning designation?

If you know the relevant zoning designation for the property:

  • Go to: VMC Title 20 Land Use and Development Code.
  • Find applicable zoning district chapter.
  • Click on 'Development Standards' to determine setbacks and building height.

Do I need a permit for a shed?

If you are building a shed 120 square feet or less a permit is not required. However, you must still comply with VMC 20.902. Fences, Decks, Sheds

Do I need a permit for a fence?

Fences 6’ in height or less do not require a permit. However, you must still comply with VMC 20.912. Fences, Decks, Sheds

Do I need a building permit for a detached garage/shop?

Yes, structures over 120 square feet require a building permit and a land use review to verify setbacks, building height and maximum building lot coverage. Residential Building Permits

Do I need a permit to replace an existing driveway or pour a RV pad?

Yes, a land use permit is required to replace an existing driveway or pour a new RV parking pad. A right-of-way permit may also be required if any work is done within the City’s right-of-way.

An Over the Counter (PDF) land use permit application and plot plan is required to be submitted at the Permit Center.

Refer to Engineering Services for right-of-way information.

Are accessory dwelling units (ADUs) allowed in the City of Vancouver?

Yes, ADUs are allowed in compliance with VMC 20.810. Information on ADUs may be found on our Accessory Dwelling Units page.

Do I need a permit to cut trees on my property?

Most tree removals require a permit. If you wish to remove a tree or perform major pruning, visit our Tree Permits webpage for guidance.

Can I operate a business out of my home?

In the City of Vancouver an individual is allowed to operate certain types of businesses from their residence. Visit our Home Occupation Permit page for more information.

Can I have a Family Day Care Home at my residence?

If you care for 12 or fewer children in your home, this would be an allowed use.

How many animals can I have on my property?

Please refer to VMC 8.24 Animals for requirements.
