Positions open June 1-30, 2022 for experienced lateral firefighters. 

The Vancouver Fire Department is committed to safeguarding lives, property, and the environment within our community. With 10 stations (an 11th station currently being built) and almost 200 fire suppression personnel, the Vancouver Fire Department responds to over 28,000 calls per year.

Vancouver firefighters serve the community in many ways including fighting fires, providing emergency medical care through our state–of-the-art Emergency Medical Service (EMS) program, responding to hazardous materials incidents, performing on specialty response teams, and providing education to the community. Vancouver firefighters work as a team to provide the community with professional, effective, and compassionate service.

 

Qualifications, Compensation and Benefits

Minimum requirements

Entry Level Firefighter

  • 21 years at application closing date
  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Valid driver’s license
  • Firefighting experience is desired but not required
  • Successful completion of CPAT
  • Have or obtain WA EMT-B certification within one year of hire

Starting Salary: $80,172

 

Entry Level Firefighter Paramedic

  • 21 years at application closing date
  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Valid driver’s license
  • Firefighting experience is desired but not required
  • Successful completion of CPAT
  • Nationally Registered Paramedic
  • While not required, Washington State and Clark County certified Paramedics are desired

Starting Salary: $97,284

 

Lateral Firefighter-EMT Basic (Paramedic Preferred)

  • 21 years at application closing date
  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Valid driver’s license
  • 3 Years full time firefighting experience is required
  • Washington State Firefighter 1 certification or equivalent
  • WA EMT-B certification (Paramedic preferred)
  • Washington State requires all Firefighter and EMS must be fully vaccinated by October 18, 2021 or provide a signed religious or medical exemption.

Salary Range: $80,172-$124,644 depending on qualifications

 

Benefits

  • Firefighters work a schedule of 24 hour shifts with multiple days off per week
  • Paid vacation and sick leave accrual
  • Comprehensive medical, dental, vision, and prescription coverage for employee and family
  • Competitive public safety retirement plan – Washington State LEOFF II

  • Law Enforcement Officers’ and Fire Fighters’ (LEOFF) Retirement System*

    LEOFF Plan 2 is a defined benefit plan. When you meet plan requirements and retire, you are guaranteed a monthly benefit for the rest of your life.

    What is a defined benefit?

    A retirement plan in which your benefit is based on a formula rather than an account balance. The formula provides a monthly benefit based on your years of service and your Final Average Salary.

    Your monthly benefit will be based on your earned service credit and compensation while a member of LEOFF Plan 2. This formula will be used to calculate your monthly benefit:

    2% x service credit years x Final Average Salary = monthly benefit

    You, your employer and the state each contribute a percentage of your salary or wages to help fund the plan. The LEOFF Plan 2 Retirement Board adopts contribution rates and periodically adjusts them to reflect the overall cost of the plan.

    You are vested in the plan when you have five years of service credit. Once you are vested, you have earned the right to a future monthly benefit. If you leave your job and withdraw your contributions, however, you give up your right to a benefit.

    You are eligible to retire with a full benefit at age 53 if you have at least five years of service credit. Retirement before 53 is considered an early retirement. If you have at least 20 years of service credit and are at least age 50, you can choose to retire early, but your benefit might be reduced.

    If the unexpected happens — disability or death before retirement — a benefit might be available. If you become totally incapacitated and leave your job as a result, you might be eligible for a disability retirement benefit.

    If you die before you retire, your spouse, registered domestic partner or minor child, if applicable, could be eligible to receive a benefit based on your years of service credit. For more information visit: www.drs.wa.gov/oaa *Information from LEOFF 2 Handbook

Application Process

 

To Apply

The application period for experienced lateral firefighter and all entry level positions is closed. Stay tuned for future position announcements.

  • The Vancouver Fire Department utilizes Public Safety Testing for all Entry Level positions. Entry level applicants must complete a written exam through Public Safety testing and a pass the CPAT in order to apply. Applicants must also by able to successfully complete a panel interview, thorough background investigation, Chief's interview, and psychological and medical testing. Visit the link below for further information on Public Safety Testing.
  • Applicants for lateral firefighter submit an application, resume and cover letter through the City Of Vancouver when positions are announced. No written exam or CPAT is required. Applicants must be able to successfully complete a thorough background investigation, Chief's Interview, psychological and medical testing.

About the City of Vancouver

The City of Vancouver is the fourth largest city in the state of Washington. Located directly across from Portland, Oregon on the North bank of the Columbia River, Vancouver combines the excitement of a major metropolitan area with small town charm. Vancouver offers quality schools, a favorable tax climate, affordable housing, livable neighborhoods, and an abundance of recreational opportunities.
