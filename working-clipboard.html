Utilizing the policy guidance provided by City Council, the 2016-2021 Strategic Plan and the City's Complete Streets ordinance, and the Transportation System Plan the City continues to implement projects and programs to improve equity, safety and mobility for all users.

Capital Projects

Through partnerships with the City's Pavement Management Program, the utilization of grants, local funds, and other available resources, the City continues to implement capital projects throughout the City.

Completed

  • Tech Center Drive: In the summer of 2021, the City of Vancouver completed a safety improvement project on SE Tech Center Drive between SE 172nd Avenue and SE Tech Center Place/SE 167th Avenue. The project added protected bicycle facilities, new pedestrian crossings, and traffic calming.

In Progress

  • McLoughlin Boulevard: In 2019, the City improvements on McLoughlin Boulevard between Reserve Street and Brandt Road. These improvements included both permanent speed cushions and flashing light intersection crossings, as well as “pilot” buffered bike lanes and sharrows (shared lane bicycle markings).
  • Columbia Street Safety and Mobility Project (Westside Bike Mobility Project): In the summer of 2021 the City will paved Columbia Street from Mill Plain Boulevard to 45th Street, and put down a microsurfacing treatment from Mill Plain Boulevard south to Columbia Way. This provided an opportunity to add safety and mobility elements uch as protected bicycle facilities, signal upgrades, pedestrian crossings, traffic calming, and street trees.
  • E Fourth Plain Boulevard between Main Street and Andresen Road: Fourth Plain Boulevard will have an asphalt rubber chip seal placed down in 2023 from Main Street to Andresen Road. The pavement project will provide an opportunity to evaluate potential changes to the street to improve safety, mobility, and accessibility through the repurposing of a travel lane.
  • SE 34th Street between SE 164th Avenue and the city limits at SE 192nd Avenue: SE 34th Street will have a microsurfacing treatment in 2022 from SE 164th Avenue to SE 192nd Avenue. The pavement project will provide an opportunity to evaluate potential changes to the street to improve safety, mobility, and accessibility and repurposing a travel lane to add bicycle facilities.

Collision Information

To better understand safety trends and issues on the transportation system, the City continues to complete plans, studies, and tools to utilize collision data.

  • In 2018 the City completed its first comprehensive collision analysis, known as the Transportation System Safety Analysis (TSSA). The project focused on developing a baseline analysis for citywide collisions and tools to help the City proactively address current and future priority collision locations and patterns.
  • Building on the TSSA, Vancouver Moves is an update to the City's first Transportation System Plan that was adopted in 2004. As a first step, the project examined existing travel conditions for all users of the city's transportation system, including those who drive, walk, bicycle, ride transit or deliver freight.
