Neighborhood Associations

Although they are often confused, neighborhood associations are NOT the same as homeowner associations.

Neighborhood associations are independent organizations that bring neighbors together to work with the City of Vancouver to address neighborhood problems, share information, provide opportunities for community input, and undertake projects to benefit the neighborhood. The city has built strong partnerships between city hall and the community through relationships with recognized neighborhood associations.

Vancouver has 69 officially recognized neighborhood associations that span over 95 percent of the city's population and geography. Membership in a neighborhood association includes everyone who owns property, rents a home, lives and/or works in the neighborhood boundaries. If you live or work in the City of Vancouver, it is very likely that you belong to a neighborhood association!

Neighborhood Association Meetings

Most neighborhood associations have regular meetings of the membership. Meeting agendas are set by the Neighborhood Association officers. Check the individual neighborhood association pages for newsletters with more information.

Office of Neighborhoods

The Office of Neighborhoods provides City residents, City Council and staff with responsive, coordinated services that promote and support collaboration and communication among residents and City government through recognized neighborhood associations.
